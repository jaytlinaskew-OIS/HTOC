Here’s a step-by-step roadmap to go from your raw data (dates + indicators) to a trained model that predicts whether an indicator will reappear in 7 days.

---

## **STEP-BY-STEP PLAN**

### **Step 1: Load and Clean Your Data**
Make sure your data looks like:

| date       | indicator      |
|------------|----------------|
| 2025-03-01 | 192.168.1.1    |
| 2025-03-01 | 10.0.0.5       |
| 2025-03-02 | 192.168.1.1    |
| ...        | ...            |

- Ensure date is a `datetime` column
- Remove duplicates
- Sort by `indicator` and `date`

---

### **Step 2: Create Your Target Labels**
For each row (indicator, date), label:
> **1 if the same indicator appears again within the next 7 days**, else **0**

Approach:
- For each `(indicator, date)`, look ahead 7 days and check if that indicator reappears

---

### **Step 3: Create the Modeling Dataset**
Now, build a row for each `(indicator, date)` that has sufficient history behind it. This is your modeling base.

---

### **Step 4: Feature Engineering**
For each `(indicator, date)` row, generate features from history:

- `days_since_last_seen`
- `seen_in_last_3d`, `7d`, `14d`, `30d`
- `first_seen_date` and `days_since_first_seen`
- `total_seen_count` up to this point
- `consecutive_active_days`
- Optionally: `day_of_week` or `day_of_month` as features

Store these features with the target label.

---

### **Step 5: Train-Test Split (Time-Aware)**
Split your data chronologically:
- Train on earlier data (e.g., all before March)
- Validate on later data (e.g., March onward)

Avoid random splits—use time-based slicing.

---

### **Step 6: Train a Model**
Use:
- **XGBoost or LightGBM**: for best performance and speed
- Optionally: **logistic regression** to interpret importance

Train with your engineered features and the binary labels.

---

### **Step 7: Evaluate**
Use:
- **Precision / Recall / F1-Score**
- **ROC-AUC**
- **Confusion Matrix** to understand false positives vs. false negatives

Focus on recall if missing an indicator is costly, or precision if false positives are expensive.

---

### **Step 8: Predict**
Now you can take today’s indicator list, extract features, and predict:
> Which indicators are likely to return in the next 7 days?

This becomes your **early warning system**.

---

### **Optional: Deploy / Monitor**
Once working, you can:
- Automate daily predictions
- Monitor model drift (behavior changing over time)
- Consider retraining weekly or monthly
